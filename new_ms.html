FAQs

  1. How is Georgia Tech?

    2. Its Great ! It is ranked 10th in CS (this page). COE rankings for other engineering departments.

    In CS, we have many research areas and centers. Our labs are better than most colleges i have been to.

  2. Fees / Living costs?

    3. Fees are around 12K a semester. Fall & Spring = 24$ a year for fees. Summer semester is optional and people go for internships at that time. But if you register for courses in summer, you'll have to pay fees for the courses you take.
    Check latest fees details on the bursar's office website

    Living costs are around $450-500 if you share a room and stay off-campus. On campus rates are higher (around $600-800 + food extra).

  3. Why does my i20 list a huge figure for expenses?

    4. Well, that is an (over) exaggerated amout. Actual living costs are quite less if you share your room, live off-campus, cook your own food etc. The i20 amount is for Visa purposes and you need to have that much amount as liquid assets at the time of your visa interview.

  4. Funding ?

    5. Funding is not guaranteed. You can get funded as a GRA - Graduate Research Assistant (or as a GTA-teaching asst. in some dept excluding CS). It is the professor who hires you. So, you need to convince him/her that you're capable of doing good research. Typically one would take a special problem (individual project - CS8903 - unpaid) under the professor for a semester and do a good job. If the professor likes your work and has a GRA opening, he might hire you or else recommend you to someone who has an opening.

    Advise on how to contact professors: Write them a directed email and ask for appointment. (Do not spam professors). By directed email, i mean tailored for him/her. You should be aware of his research before you write to him. Typically, professors look for bright students who are interested in their resarch areas and have some work in the area before. Some expect you to have a couple of papers/projects in that field or do well in a particular class or to have relevant work-experience. After you have an appointment, meet the professor to discuss possibilities of research as a GRA or special problem.

  5. Oncampus/Offcampus Jobs

    6. You are allowed to work for 20 hours oncampus. Payscales vary from $6-$20 per hr depending on the type of job. To get an on-campus job, you need to hunt for it at various departments/offices on campus. There are some mailing lists on lists.gatech.edu and mailman.cc.gatech.edu for job postings.

    Typical jobs: Programming jobs and grader positions have good salaries (12-20$ an hr). Some people get some low paying jobs ($6-10) like the ones at CRC (referee, lifeguard), mail sorting, lab/library admin or assistant etc.

    International students are not allowed to work off-campus for the first 9 months. After that you can get an approval from OIE (which requires a letter from grad advisor) and CPT (curricular practical training) authorization that allows you to work part-time. Internship is also under CPT but it is fulltime. OPT (Optional practical training) is applicable for working in the US after graduation.

  6. Placements/Jobs after graduation

    7. Gatech Career services organizes many fairs and has a better placement record than many other schools. Apart from the general career fair, the CS and ECE depertments have a separate career fair. This year (2008) we started a career fair for Information Security students. Many local student groups organize career fairs (GT Alumni assoc, Black grad student assoc etc.). There are regular info-sessions from companies at gatech. The career services organizes information and resume-review sessions for all gatech students. CS department organizes a resume-review session which brings recruiters from companies to give you advise on how to make your resume stronger. GTISC has been organizing a session for building interviewing skills and office etiquette.

    Almost everyone i know in CS who maintains a decent GPA (above 3.5) has a job/internship or ongoing interviews.

  7. Indians@Gatech

    8. Gatech has an Indian student's club that holds activities throughout the year. Website; Yahoo group: ic_gt at yahoogroups.com; Facebook page

    There are lots of Indians staying in Atlanta (mainly in Decatur, Alpharetta, Duluth areas). So you have many indian temples, restaurants, grocery stores etc around.

  8. Transportation around campus

    9. Gatech has Buses (Stinger and Tech Trolley) to go around campus. To go out, you can take a Marta Bus or Train. You can also become a zipcar member and rent cars by the hour.



  9. When are the admits sent (generally)?

    10. MS admits are sent around late April to early May. PhD admits are sent by end of Feb or early March

Misc

  1. How to call back home?

    2. I use Localphone.com. There are several calling card providers for India that you can try (Tata, Reliance, Airtel, Google Voice etc).

  2. Where to hangout?

    3. Atlantic Station, Westside district, Midtown, Buckhead are decent places to hangout.

    Lookup yelp.com, google maps, urbanspoon.com and scoutmob apps for information on restaurants etc.

  3. Where to buy food?

    4. Publix, Kroger, Walmart, Trader Joes and Whole Foods (expensive) are local grocery stores. Look them up on Google Maps.

    Indian food/spices can be bought from the 14th St grocery (facebook page). In Atlanta, there are a lot of Indian stores in the Decatur area. See Patel bros, Cherians
